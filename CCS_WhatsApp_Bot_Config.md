## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: How much paint do I need for one room?**
As a rough guide, 1 litre covers about 100-120 sq. ft. in one coat. A standard 10x12 ft room (walls only) usually needs about 8-10 litres for two coats. For an exact estimate, tell us your room size, or visit the shop.

**Q: How many coats of paint are needed?**
Most walls need a primer/putty base plus two coats of paint for an even, long-lasting finish. Dark or bold shades may need an extra coat.

**Q: What is the difference between interior and exterior paint?**
Exterior paints resist sun, rain, and dust, and prevent fading and algae. Interior paints focus on smooth finish, easy cleaning, and low odour. Never use interior paint outside.

**Q: How long does paint take to dry?**
Surface drying is usually 30 minutes to 1 hour. Wait 4-6 hours between coats. Allow the wall to fully cure for a few days before heavy cleaning.

**Q: Which finish should I choose — matt, satin, or gloss?**
Matt hides wall imperfections and looks elegant (bedrooms, living rooms). Satin/sheen is easy to wipe clean (kids' rooms, kitchens). Gloss/high-gloss is durable and bright (doors, trims, wood).

## COLOUR SUGGESTIONS GUIDE (2026 TRENDS)

Always ask about the room, lighting, and existing furniture before recommending.

**Overall 2026 direction:** Indian homes in 2026 are moving towards warm, earthy, soft and calm palettes — colours that feel natural and restful. There is a clear shift away from cool greys towards warm neutrals.

**Living room** — Warm neutrals are the trending choice: beige, almond cream, soft walnut, light mushroom grey. They blend well with wooden furniture and traditional decor. For a feature wall, suggest terracotta or deep clay. "Colour drenching" (walls, ceiling and trims in one warm neutral) is trending for a cosy, cocoon-like feel.

**Bedroom** — Calming, nature-inspired tones: sage and moss green pair well with light wood and plants. For a cosy effect, rich shades like burgundy and deep red are popular for 2026. Soft pastels also work for a light, restful look.

**Kids' room / nursery** — Soft pastels: pastel lavender, powder blue, blush pink, mint green. They make rooms feel bigger, lighter and cheerful.

**Kitchen** — Warm, easy-to-clean shades in satin finish. Terracotta and warm beige work well with neutral cabinetry or stone textures.

**Bathroom** — Fresh, clean tones: watery teals and clear blues create a clean, refreshing, coastal feel.

**Small rooms / apartments (1BHK, 2BHK)** — Light tones that reflect light and feel airy: soft whites, pale pastels, light warm neutrals.

**Exterior walls** — Durable, weather-resistant shades. Earthy terracotta and warm beige are popular as they hide dust well. Always suggest a proven exterior product for sun and monsoon protection.

**Wood (doors, furniture, panelling)** — Suggest ICA, Sirca, Wembley, or Duco. A trending technique is "shadow painting" — finishing panelled doors, beams or arches 10-20% darker than the surrounding wall for a subtle architectural look.

**Designer decor accents** — For statement walls suggest Nilaya Wallpaper, Italian Textures, or Suzuki Luxuture Textures. Mixing finishes — a matt wall with satin or gloss trims — gives a refined, premium, layered look.
